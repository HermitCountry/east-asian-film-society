East Asian Film Society

The East Asian Film Society was founded in Honolulu, HI as a way of bringing difficult to find East Asian films on the campus of University of Hawai`i at Mānoa. The scope was soon expanded to provide screenings for the entire Honolulu community. Grants were awarded by the SEED, the Associated Students Union of UH, and the East Asian Council. Additionally the Center for Korean Studies at UH Mānoa hired us to run two semesters of film programs of South Korean films.

Rights Management

Negotiating fees and permissions to screen foreign films can be difficult, confusing, and time consuming; especially in the case of obscure or lesser known films. With our experience bringing films to the US region from East Asia and through our working relationships with several governmental organizations including the Korean Film Archive, the Hong Kong Film Archive and the Chinese Taipei Film Archive we are able to ensure that all necessary permissions are granted and that filmmakers and stakeholders receive compensation for the film screenings, if applicable.

We specialize in organizing screenings for non-profit and educational institutions and have worked closely with and sought advice from legal departments at major US institutions to ensure that our screenings do not violate any copyright laws. We ensure that the legal status of each film we screen is given in writing and additionally provide documentation of all payments to the rightsholders.

We strive to take the guess work out of the increasingly complicated and confusing task of arranging film screenings so you can focus on the films themselves!

Promotional Material

Creating professional-looking promotional material and accurate film synopses and information is a time-consuming task. We can work closely with you to provide you a variety of print and online promotional material: from large posters, to fliers, to text that you can use in your own online newsletters, we are able to provide a variety of services to help you advertise and promote your events.

From a single screening for a dozen people to a quarter-long film series for hundreds of people, we have experience promoting events of all scales. For large organizations and institutions with internal publications departments we are able to provide print-quality graphics, frame enlargements, accurate film synopses and information and extensive background material to use in your existing newsletters and publications. For smaller organizations we are able to to create print and online ads from scratch to promote your event.

We can save you time and effort by providing print-quality graphics, accurate information, and extensive background material for each film we screen!

Guest Lectures

We are happy to arrange for Professors, Graduate Students or experts in the field to provide insightful introductions for any of the films we screen. From senior Professors to actors and actresses we have experience arranging for informative and insightful introductions to the films we screen as well as Question and Answer sessions following the films. We are happy to arrange for introductions and Q&A sessions for any size group.

If you are interested in introducing the films yourselves or have someone in mind to introduce the films we can help by providing extensive background information as well as a relevant bibliography to assist with preparing an introduction. Our goal is to provide as much information about these films as possible to make the experience educational as well as enjoyable.
